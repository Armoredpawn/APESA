Political Processes and Ideological Themes

Unit Overview

This unit explores the governmental policies, wars, and conflicts that have shaped the modern borders and governments of Southern Africa. We will explore the decolonization of the region, and the political effects of the apartheid, with a case study on the African national Congress. Alongside this, we will study the different ideologies and resistance against pan-africanism. We will also explore the processes used by the governments today.

4.2: Political Effects of Apartheid

This section will explore the discriminatory government of Southern Africa during the Apartheid, as well as the rise of resistance groups and how they combatted segregation. There will be a case study of the African National Congress to help convey these ideas.

4.3: Ideological differences in Southern Africa

This section will explore various conflicting ideas within the region, such as Pan-Africanism vs. ethnonationalism and Capitalism vs. socialism. There will be a case study of resistance groups against Pan-Africanism ideals.

4.4: Modern-day governmental processes

This section will focus on contemporary political processes within the region, such as rising and declining democratization patterns, as well as how they affect the rest of the world on a global scale.

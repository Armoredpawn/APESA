The Movement and Concentration of Peoples in Southern Africa

Unit Overview

This unit will focus on the spatial patterns and distribution of population clusters and cohorts within the region. Factors affecting population growth will also be discussed. We will examine the population pyramids of the region and its individual countries. In addition, we will explore the region’s demographic transition model and analyze shifts in population over time.

2.1: Population Density Patterns

This section will explore the distribution, density, and cohorts of the Southern Africa population. There will be a case study of the region’s population pyramid to visually show population patterns.

2.2: Population Growth and Decline

This section covers the factors that could cause the growth or decline of the Southern African population, such as diseases, food supply, and fertility rates. To emphasize these concepts, there will be a case study of this region’s demographic transition model.

2.3: Interregional and Intraregional Migration

This section will explore one of the largest factors of population growth and decline, migration. The study would delve into push and pull factors, as well as popular migration destinations.
